Information School		 Project: Implementation and evaluation of a retro recommendation website
Researchers:

Xinyu Shi (xshi28@sheffield.ac.uk);
Alessandro Checco (a.checco@sheffield.ac.uk)

Purpose of the research:

The aims of this project are to implement a retro recommendation website, and propose relevant optimization suggestions based on analysis and evaluation. The website will allow users to enter the names of celebrities from the past (the 20th century), and recommend relevant pictures based on the photos of the celebrity.

Who will be participating?

We are inviting adults over 18 to participate in the project.

What will you be asked to do?

You will be asked to search for specific keywords, complete specific tasks, and answer the corresponding questions on the questionnaire; Then try more keyword searches and other operations according to your interests and answer the remaining questions. The whole process takes about 20 minutes.

What are the potential risks of participating?

The risks of participating are the same as those experienced in everyday life.

What data will we collect?

The project does not collect any information related to personal identity. The questionnaire including a series of open questions and closed questions related to the retro recommendation website, the information we will collect is about the experience, feelings, etc., so it will not involve any personally identifiable information.

What will we do with the data?

I will be analyzing the data for inclusion in my Masters dissertation. The data will stored on the Information School's research data drive which can be accessed only by me, my supervisor, and the School's Examinations Officer and ICT staff operating the facility. I will also store an encrypted password protected back up copy on my personal laptop.

Will my participation be confidential?

I will anonymise all data before analysis. No identifying information will be retained.

What will happen to the results of the research project?

The results of this study will be included in my master’s dissertation which will be publicly available.

What is the legal basis for processing your personal data?

The University of Sheffield will act as the Data Controller for this study. This means that the University is responsible for looking after your information and using it properly. In order to collect and use your personal information as part of this research project, we must have a basis in law to do so. The basis that we are using is that the research is ‘a task in the public interest’.
